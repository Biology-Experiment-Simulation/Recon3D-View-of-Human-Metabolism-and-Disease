CC   -!- FUNCTION: Non-receptor serine/threonine protein kinase which is
CC       necessary for the maintenance of skeletal muscle structure and
CC       function. May play a role in myocyte differentiation and survival
CC       by regulating the integrity of the nuclear envelope and the
CC       expression of muscle-specific genes. May also phosphorylate
CC       PPP1R12A and inhibit the myosin phosphatase activity to regulate
CC       myosin phosphorylation. Also critical to the modulation of cardiac
CC       contractility and to the maintenance of proper cardiac conduction
CC       activity probably through the regulation of cellular calcium
CC       homeostasis. Phosphorylates PLN, a regulator of calcium pumps and
CC       may regulate sarcoplasmic reticulum calcium uptake in myocytes.
CC       May also phosphorylate FXYD1/PLM which is able to induce chloride
CC       currents. May also play a role in synaptic plasticity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10811636, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913253,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11287000, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15598648,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21457715, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21949239}.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Dystrophia myotonica 1 (DM1) [MIM:160900]: A muscular

CC       extremities, cataract, hypogonadism, defective endocrine

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1302022, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1310900,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1546326, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19514047}. Note=The
CC       disease is caused by mutations affecting the gene represented in
CC       this entry. The causative mutation is a CTG expansion in the 3'-
CC       UTR of the DMPK gene. A length exceeding 50 CTG repeats is
CC       pathogenic, while normal individuals have 5 to 37 repeats.
CC       Intermediate alleles with 35-49 triplets are not disease-causing
CC       but show instability in intergenerational transmissions. Disease
CC       severity varies with the number of repeats: mildly affected
CC       persons have 50 to 150 repeats, patients with classic DM have 100
CC       to 1,000 repeats, and those with congenital onset can have more
CC       than 2,000 repeats. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1310900,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19514047}.
