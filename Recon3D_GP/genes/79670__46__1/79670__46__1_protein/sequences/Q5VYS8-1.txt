CC   -!- FUNCTION: Uridylyltransferase that mediates the terminal
CC       uridylation of mRNAs with short (less than 25 nucleotides) poly(A)
CC       tails, hence facilitating global mRNA decay (PubMed:19703396,
CC       PubMed:25480299). Involved in microRNA (miRNA)-induced gene
CC       silencing through uridylation of deadenylated miRNA targets
CC       (PubMed:25480299). Also acts as a suppressor of miRNA biogenesis
CC       by mediating the terminal uridylation of some miRNA precursors,
CC       including that of let-7 (pre-let-7). Uridylated pre-let-7 RNA is
CC       not processed by Dicer and undergo degradation. Pre-let-7
CC       uridylation is strongly enhanced in the presence of LIN28A
CC       (PubMed:22898984). Due to functional redundancy between ZCCHC6 and
CC       ZCCHC11, the identification of the specific role of each of these
CC       proteins is difficult. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19703396,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22898984, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25480299}.
