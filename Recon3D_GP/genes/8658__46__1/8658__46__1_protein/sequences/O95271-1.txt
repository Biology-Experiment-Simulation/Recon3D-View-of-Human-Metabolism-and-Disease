CC   -!- FUNCTION: Poly-ADP-ribosyltransferase involved in various
CC       processes such as Wnt signaling pathway, telomere length and
CC       vesicle trafficking. Acts as an activator of the Wnt signaling
CC       pathway by mediating poly-ADP-ribosylation (PARsylation) of AXIN1
CC       and AXIN2, 2 key components of the beta-catenin destruction
CC       complex: poly-ADP-ribosylated target proteins are recognized by
CC       RNF146, which mediates their ubiquitination and subsequent
CC       degradation. Also mediates PARsylation of BLZF1 and CASC3,
CC       followed by recruitment of RNF146 and subsequent ubiquitination.
CC       Mediates PARsylation of TERF1, thereby contributing to the
CC       regulation of telomere length. Involved in centrosome maturation
CC       during prometaphase by mediating PARsylation of HEPACAM2/MIKI. May
CC       also regulate vesicle trafficking and modulate the subcellular
CC       distribution of SLC2A4/GLUT4-vesicles. May be involved in spindle
CC       pole assembly through PARsylation of NUMA1. Stimulates 26S
CC       proteasome activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10988299,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11739745, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16076287,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19759537, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21478859,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22864114, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23622245}.

CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00397,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19759537}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Specifically inhibited by XAV939, a small
CC       molecule, leading to inhibit the Wnt signaling pathway by
CC       stabilizing AXIN1 and AXIN2. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19759537}.
