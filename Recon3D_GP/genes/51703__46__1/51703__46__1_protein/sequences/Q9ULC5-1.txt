CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acyl-CoA synthetases (ACSL) activate long-chain fatty
CC       acids for both synthesis of cellular lipids, and degradation via
CC       beta-oxidation. ACSL5 may activate fatty acids from exogenous
CC       sources for the synthesis of triacylglycerol destined for
CC       intracellular storage (By similarity). Utilizes a wide range of
CC       saturated fatty acids with a preference for C16-C18 unsaturated
CC       fatty acids (By similarity). It was suggested that it may also
CC       stimulate fatty acid oxidation (By similarity). At the villus tip
CC       of the crypt-villus axis of the small intestine may sensitize
CC       epithelial cells to apoptosis specifically triggered by the death
CC       ligand TRAIL. May have a role in the survival of glioma cells.
CC       {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17681178,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18806831, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19459852}.
