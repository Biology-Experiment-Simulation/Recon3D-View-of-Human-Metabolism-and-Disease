CC   -!- FUNCTION: Alpha-2,6-sialyltransferase involved in the synthesis of
CC       alpha-series gangliosides. Has activity toward GD1a, GT1b and
CC       GM1b. Has no activity toward glycoproteins. Responsible for the
CC       biosynthesis of DSGG (disialylgalactosylgloboside) from MSGG

CC       Participates in the synthesis of disialyl Lewis a (Le(a)).

CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The carbohydrate antigen disialyl Lewis a, which is
CC       at least partly synthesized by ST6GALNAC6, is a normal counterpart
CC       of sialyl Lewis a, better known as CA19-9, an antigen widely used
CC       as a serum marker for diagnosis of cancers in the digestive track.
CC       Disialyl Lewis a is predominantly expressed in non-malignant
CC       epithelial cells of the digestive organs, while sialyl Lewis a is
CC       preferentially expressed in cancers. Disialyl Lewis a in normal
CC       epithelial cells serves as a ligand for immunosuppressive
CC       receptors, such as SIGLEC7 and SIGLEC9, expressed on resident
CC       monocytes/macrophages and maintains immunological homeostasis of
CC       mucosal membranes in digestive organs. Sialyl Lewis a, as well as
CC       its positional isomer sialyl Lewis x, serves as a ligand for
CC       vascular cell adhesion molecule E-selectin and facilitates
CC       hematogenous metastasis through mediating adhesion of circulating
CC       cancer cells to vascular endothelium (PubMed:17760270).
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:17760270}.
