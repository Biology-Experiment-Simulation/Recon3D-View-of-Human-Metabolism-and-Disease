CC   -!- FUNCTION: May play a role in male spermatogenesis. In vitro acts
CC       as inhibitor of MGAT1 activity causing cell surface proteins to
CC       carry mainly high mannose N-glycans. The function is mediated by
CC       its lumenal domain and occurs specifically in the Golgi. A
CC       catalytic glucosyltransferase activity is not detected. May be
CC       involved in regulation of Sertoli-germ cell interactions during
CC       specific stages of spermatogenesis.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D4R2}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: May self-associate; specifically in the endoplasmic
CC       reticulum prior to its translocation to the Golgi. Interacts with
CC       MGAT1, MGAT3 and MAN2A2; may interact with MGTA1 specifically in
CC       the Golgi. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D4R2, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Golgi apparatus membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q4V8F8}; Single-pass type II membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q4V8F8}. Endoplasmic reticulum
CC       membrane {ECO:0000305}; Single-pass type II membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in testis. Poorly expressed in
CC       testis biopsies from men with impaired spermatogenesis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16773575, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26371870}.
