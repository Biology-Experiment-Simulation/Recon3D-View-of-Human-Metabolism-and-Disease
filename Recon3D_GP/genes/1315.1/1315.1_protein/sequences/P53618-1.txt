CC   -!- FUNCTION: The coatomer is a cytosolic protein complex that binds
CC       to dilysine motifs and reversibly associates with Golgi non-
CC       clathrin-coated vesicles, which further mediate biosynthetic
CC       protein transport from the ER, via the Golgi up to the trans Golgi
CC       network. Coatomer complex is required for budding from Golgi
CC       membranes, and is essential for the retrograde Golgi-to-ER
CC       transport of dilysine-tagged proteins. In mammals, the coatomer
CC       can only be recruited by membranes associated to ADP-ribosylation
CC       factors (ARFs), which are small GTP-binding proteins; the complex
CC       also influences the Golgi structural integrity, as well as the
CC       processing, activity, and endocytic recycling of LDL receptors.
CC       Plays a functional role in facilitating the transport of kappa-
CC       type opioid receptor mRNAs into axons and enhances translation of
CC       these proteins. Required for limiting lipid storage in lipid
CC       droplets. Involved in lipid homeostasis by regulating the presence
CC       of perilipin family members PLIN2 and PLIN3 at the lipid droplet
CC       surface and promoting the association of adipocyte surface
CC       triglyceride lipase (PNPLA2) with the lipid droplet to mediate
CC       lipolysis (By similarity). Involved in the Golgi disassembly and
CC       reassembly processes during cell cycle. Involved in autophagy by
CC       playing a role in early endosome function. Plays a role in
CC       organellar compartmentalization of secretory compartments
CC       including endoplasmic reticulum (ER)-Golgi intermediate
CC       compartment (ERGIC), Golgi, trans-Golgi network (TGN) and
CC       recycling endosomes, and in biosynthetic transport of CAV1.
CC       Promotes degradation of Nef cellular targets CD4 and MHC class I
CC       antigens by facilitating their trafficking to degradative
CC       compartments. {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18385291,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18725938, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19364919,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20056612}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Oligomeric complex that consists of at least the alpha,
CC       beta, beta', gamma, delta, epsilon and zeta subunits. Interacts
CC       (via C-terminus) with HIV-1 Nef; the interaction is direct.
CC       Interacts with SCYL1. Interacts with COPG1. Interacts (via trunk
CC       domain) with ARF1 (via switch I region); the interaction is
CC       direct. Interacts with KCNK2/TREK (via N-terminus); this
CC       interaction increases the channel-mediated whole cell currents and
CC       promotes plasma membrane expression of KCNK2/TREK. Interacts with
CC       anthrax lethal factor (LF); this interaction may facilitate
CC       endosomal vesicle membrane translocation of LF and its release
CC       from the lumen of endosomal vesicles to external milieu. Interacts
CC       with CAPN8 and PRKCE (By similarity). Interacts with ARF1
CC       (myristoylated); this interaction is required for binding of COPB1
CC       to Golgi membranes (By similarity). Interacts with STX17 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with TMEM115. {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24806965}.

CC       or polymerized on the cytoplasmic side of the Golgi, as well as on
CC       the vesicles/buds originating from it (By similarity). Proteolytic
CC       cleavage by CAPN8 triggers translocation from Golgi to cytoplasm
CC       (By similarity). Found in perinuclear vesicular-tubular clusters
CC       (VTCs) and in the Golgi region where associated with vesicles,
CC       buds and rims of the Golgi stack (By similarity). Occasionally
CC       present at the trans-side of Golgi, but mainly present at the cis-
CC       Golgi side in transitional areas (TA), on so-called peripheral
CC       elements (PE) consisting of tubules and vesicles located between
CC       the cup-shaped transitional elements (TE) of the rough endoplasmic
CC       reticulum (RER) and the cis-most Golgi cisternae (By similarity).
CC       Present in cytoplasm, not associated with visible coats or
CC       membranes, with a minor fraction present on small clusters of
CC       tubules and vesicles (By similarity). Some association with high-
CC       density and low-density microsomes and mitochondria/nuclei
CC       fraction (By similarity). Very little found in plasma membrane
CC       fraction (PubMed:20362547). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P23514,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20362547}.
