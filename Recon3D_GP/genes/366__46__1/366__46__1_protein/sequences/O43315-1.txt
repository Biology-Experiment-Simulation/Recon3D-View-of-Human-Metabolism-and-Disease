CC   -!- FUNCTION: Forms a channel with a broad specificity. Mediates
CC       passage of a wide variety of non-charged solutes including
CC       carbamides, polyols, purines, and pyrimidines in a phloretin- and
CC       mercury-sensitive manner, whereas amino acids, cyclic sugars,
CC       Na(+), K(+), Cl(-), and deprotonated monocarboxylates are
CC       excluded. Also permeable to urea and glycerol.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10564231}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane; Multi-pass membrane protein.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in peripheral leukocytes.
CC       Also expressed in liver, lung, and spleen.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Aquaporins contain two tandem repeats each containing
CC       three membrane-spanning domains and a pore-forming loop with the
CC       signature motif Asn-Pro-Ala (NPA).
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the MIP/aquaporin (TC 1.A.8) family.
