CC   -!- FUNCTION: Co-chaperone required during spermatogenesis to repress
CC       transposable elements and prevent their mobilization, which is
CC       essential for the germline integrity. Acts via the piRNA metabolic
CC       process, which mediates the repression of transposable elements
CC       during meiosis by forming complexes composed of piRNAs and Piwi
CC       proteins and govern the methylation and subsequent repression of
CC       transposons. Acts as a co-chaperone via its interaction with HSP90
CC       and is required for the piRNA amplification process, the secondary
CC       piRNA biogenesis. May be required together with HSP90 in removal
CC       of 16 nucleotide ping-pong by-products from Piwi complexes,
CC       possibly facilitating turnover of Piwi complexes (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250}.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Note=FKBP6 is located in the Williams-Beuren syndrome
CC       (WBS) critical region. WBS results from a hemizygous deletion of
CC       several genes on chromosome 7q11.23, thought to arise as a
CC       consequence of unequal crossing over between highly homologous
CC       low-copy repeat sequences flanking the deleted region.
CC       Haploinsufficiency of FKBP6 may be the cause of certain
CC       cardiovascular and musculo-skeletal abnormalities observed in the
CC       disease (PubMed:9782077). A father and son with Williams-Beuren
CC       syndrome appear to have a common heterozygous deletion that
CC       includes FKBP6 gene. However, the haploinsufficiency for FKBP6
CC       does not appear to preclude male fertility (PubMed:15770126).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15770126, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9782077}.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Note=Defects in FKBP6 may be a cause of azoospermia. A
CC       study based on 323 patients with azoospermia or severe
CC       oligozoospermia suggested an association between FKBP6 variants
CC       and azoospermia (PubMed:17307919). However, other studies suggest
CC       that defects in FKBP6 are not a common cause of non-obstructive
CC       azoospermia (PubMed:16227348). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16227348,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17307919}.
