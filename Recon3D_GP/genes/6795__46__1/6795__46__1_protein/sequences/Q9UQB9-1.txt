CC   -!- FUNCTION: Serine/threonine-protein kinase component of the
CC       chromosomal passenger complex (CPC), a complex that acts as a key
CC       regulator of mitosis. The CPC complex has essential functions at
CC       the centromere in ensuring correct chromosome alignment and
CC       segregation and is required for chromatin-induced microtubule
CC       stabilization and spindle assembly. Plays also a role in meiosis
CC       and more particularly in spermatogenesis. Has redundant cellular
CC       functions with AURKB and can rescue an AURKB knockdown. Like
CC       AURKB, AURKC phosphorylates histone H3 at 'Ser-10' and 'Ser-28'.
CC       AURKC phosphorylates the CPC complex subunits BIRC5/survivin and
CC       INCENP leading to increased AURKC activity. Phosphorylates TACC1,
CC       another protein involved in cell division, at 'Ser-228'.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15316025, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15499654,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15670791, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15938719,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21493633, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21531210,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27332895}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a protein = ADP + a phosphoprotein.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Okadaic acid, an inhibitor of protein
CC       phosphatase 1 (PP1), protein phosphatase 2A (PP2A) and protein
CC       phosphatase 5 (PP5), increases AURKC activity. AURKC is also
CC       stabilized through its interaction with INCENP, which acts also as
CC       an activator. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15316025}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the chromosomal passenger complex (CPC)
CC       composed of at least BIRC5/survivin, CDCA8/borealin, INCENP, AURKB
CC       or AURKC; predominantly independent AURKB- and AURKC-containing
CC       complexes exist; in the complex interacts directly with
CC       BIRC5/survivin and INCENP. Interacts with TACC1.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15316025, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15499654,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15938719, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21531210,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27332895}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus. Chromosome. Chromosome, centromere
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27332895}. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton, spindle
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15938719}. Note=Distributes in the condensed
CC       chromosomes during prophase to metaphase. After entering anaphase,
CC       there is a dissociation from separated chromosomes and a
CC       redistribution to midzone microtubules, and finally remains in the
CC       midbody during cytokinesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21531210}.
