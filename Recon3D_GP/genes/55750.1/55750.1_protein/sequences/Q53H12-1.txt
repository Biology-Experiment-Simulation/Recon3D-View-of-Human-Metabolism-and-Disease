CC   -!- FUNCTION: Lipid kinase that can phosphorylate both
CC       monoacylglycerol and diacylglycerol to form lysophosphatidic acid
CC       (LPA) and phosphatidic acid (PA), respectively. Does not
CC       phosphorylate sphingosine. Overexpression increases the formation
CC       and secretion of LPA, resulting in transactivation of EGFR and
CC       activation of the downstream MAPK signaling pathway, leading to
CC       increased cell growth. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15939762}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + acylglycerol = ADP + acyl-sn-glycerol 3-
CC       phosphate.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + 1,2-diacyl-sn-glycerol = ADP + 1,2-
CC       diacyl-sn-glycerol 3-phosphate.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Mitochondrial DNA depletion syndrome 10 (MTDPS10)

CC       cardiomyopathy, skeletal myopathy, exercise intolerance, and
CC       lactic acidosis. Mental development is normal, but affected
CC       individuals may die early from cardiomyopathy.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22277967, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22284826}.
CC       Note=The disease is caused by mutations affecting the gene

CC   -!- DISEASE: Cataract 38 (CTRCT38) [MIM:614691]: An opacification of
CC       the crystalline lens of the eye becoming evident at birth. It
CC       frequently results in visual impairment or blindness. Opacities
CC       vary in morphology, are often confined to a portion of the lens,
CC       and may be static or progressive. In general, the more posteriorly
CC       located and dense an opacity, the greater the impact on visual
CC       function. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22415731}. Note=The disease is
CC       caused by mutations affecting the gene represented in this entry.
