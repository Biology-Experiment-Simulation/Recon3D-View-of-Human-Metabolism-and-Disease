CC   -!- FUNCTION: Succinyl-CoA synthetase functions in the citric acid
CC       cycle (TCA), coupling the hydrolysis of succinyl-CoA to the
CC       synthesis of either ATP or GTP and thus represents the only step
CC       of substrate-level phosphorylation in the TCA. The alpha subunit
CC       of the enzyme binds the substrates coenzyme A and phosphate, while
CC       succinate binding and specificity for either ATP or GTP is
CC       provided by different beta subunits. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_03222}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + succinate + CoA = ADP + phosphate +
CC       succinyl-CoA. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03222}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: GTP + succinate + CoA = GDP + phosphate +
CC       succinyl-CoA. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03222}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Carbohydrate metabolism; tricarboxylic acid cycle;
CC       succinate from succinyl-CoA (ligase route): step 1/1.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03222}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer of an alpha and a beta subunit. Different
CC       beta subunits determine nucleotide specificity. Together with the
CC       ATP-specific beta subunit SUCLA2, forms an ADP-forming succinyl-
CC       CoA synthetase (A-SCS). Together with the GTP-specific beta
CC       subunit SUCLG2 forms a GDP-forming succinyl-CoA synthetase (G-
CC       SCS). {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03222}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_03222}.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Mitochondrial DNA depletion syndrome 9 (MTDPS9)
CC       [MIM:245400]: A severe disorder due to mitochondrial dysfunction.
CC       It is characterized by infantile onset of hypotonia, lactic
CC       acidosis, severe psychomotor retardation, progressive neurologic
CC       deterioration, and excretion of methylmalonic acid.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17668387, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19526370,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20453710, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20693550}.
CC       Note=The disease is caused by mutations affecting the gene
CC       represented in this entry.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the succinate/malate CoA ligase alpha
CC       subunit family. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03222}.
