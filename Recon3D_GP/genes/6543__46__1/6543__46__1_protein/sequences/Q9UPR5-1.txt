CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates the electrogenic exchange of Ca(2+) against
CC       Na(+) ions across the cell membrane, and thereby contributes to
CC       the regulation of cytoplasmic Ca(2+) levels and Ca(2+)-dependent
CC       cellular processes. Contributes to cellular Ca(2+) homeostasis in
CC       excitable cells. Contributes to the rapid decrease of cytoplasmic
CC       Ca(2+) levels back to baseline after neuronal activation, and
CC       thereby contributes to modulate synaptic plasticity, learning and
CC       memory. Plays a role in regulating urinary Ca(2+) and Na(+)
CC       excretion. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K596}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Calcium transport is down-regulated by Na(+)
CC       and stimulated by Ca(2+). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48768}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48768}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48768}. Basolateral cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K596}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8K596}. Perikaryon
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48768}. Cell projection, dendrite
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48768}. Cell projection, dendritic spine
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P48768}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The cytoplasmic Calx-beta domains bind the regulatory
CC       Ca(2+). The first Calx-beta domain can bind up to four Ca(2+)
CC       ions. The second domain can bind another two Ca(2+) ions that are
CC       essential for calcium-regulated ion exchange.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P23685}.
