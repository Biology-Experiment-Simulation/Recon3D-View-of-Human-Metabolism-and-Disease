CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as accessory component of the N-oligosaccharyl
CC       transferase (OST) complex which catalyzes the transfer of a high
CC       mannose oligosaccharide from a lipid-linked oligosaccharide donor
CC       to an asparagine residue within an Asn-X-Ser/Thr consensus motif
CC       in nascent polypeptide chains. Involved in N-glycosylation of
CC       STT3B-dependent substrates. Specifically required for the
CC       glycosylation of a subset of acceptor sites that are near cysteine
CC       residues; in this function seems to act redundantly with MAGT1. In
CC       its oxidized form proposed to form transient mixed disulfides with
CC       a glycoprotein substrate to facilitate access of STT3B to the
CC       unmodified acceptor site. Has also oxidoreductase-independent
CC       functions in the STT3B-containing OST complex possibly involving
CC       substrate recognition. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:25135935,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:12887896, ECO:0000305|PubMed:24685145}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Magnesium transporter. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19717468}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein glycosylation.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Accessory component of the STT3B-containing form of the
CC       oligosaccharyltransferase (OST) complex. OST exists in two
CC       different complex forms which contain common core subunits RPN1,
CC       RPN2, OST48, OST4, DAD1 and TMEM258, either STT3A or STT3B as
CC       catalytic subunits, and form-specific accessory subunits. OST can
CC       form stable complexes with the Sec61 complex or with both the
CC       Sec61 and TRAP complexes. The association of TUSC3 or MAGT1 with
CC       the STT3B-containing complex seems to be mutually exclusvice.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:12887896, ECO:0000305|PubMed:24685145}.
