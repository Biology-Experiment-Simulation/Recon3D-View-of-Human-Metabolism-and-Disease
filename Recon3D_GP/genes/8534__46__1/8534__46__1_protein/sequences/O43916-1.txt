CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sulfotransferase that utilizes 3'-phospho-5'-adenylyl
CC       sulfate (PAPS) as sulfonate donor to catalyze the transfer of
CC       sulfate to position 6 of galactose (Gal) residues of keratan. Has
CC       a preference for sulfating keratan sulfate, but it also transfers
CC       sulfate to the unsulfated polymer. The sulfotransferase activity
CC       on sialyl LacNAc structures is much higher than the corresponding
CC       desialylated substrate, and only internal Gal residues are
CC       sulfated. May function in the sulfation of sialyl N-
CC       acetyllactosamine oligosaccharide chains attached to
CC       glycoproteins. Participates in biosynthesis of selectin ligands.
CC       Selectin ligands are present in high endothelial cells (HEVs) and
CC       play a central role in lymphocyte homing at sites of inflammation.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10330415, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10642612}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 3'-phosphoadenylyl sulfate + keratan =
CC       adenosine 3',5'-bisphosphate + keratan 6'-sulfate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9405439}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Golgi apparatus membrane {ECO:0000250};
CC       Single-pass type II membrane protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Widely expressed at low level. Expressed in
CC       brain and skeletal muscle. Expressed by high endothelial cells
CC       (HEVs) and leukocytes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11310842,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9405439}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the sulfotransferase 1 family.
CC       Gal/GlcNAc/GalNAc subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Was originally (PubMed:9639683) thought to be the
CC       ortholog of chicken CHST3 and therefore named C6ST. However, it
CC       has no strong chondroitin 6-sulfotransferase activity.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:9639683}.
