CC   -!- FUNCTION: Essential peptidyl-tRNA hydrolase component of the
CC       mitochondrial large ribosomal subunit. Acts as a codon-independent
CC       translation release factor that has lost all stop codon
CC       specificity and directs the termination of translation in
CC       mitochondrion, possibly in case of abortive elongation. May be
CC       involved in the hydrolysis of peptidyl-tRNAs that have been
CC       prematurely terminated and thus in the recycling of stalled
CC       mitochondrial ribosomes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20186120}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: N-substituted aminoacyl-tRNA + H(2)O = N-
CC       substituted amino acid + tRNA.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the mitochondrial large ribosomal subunit
CC       (mt-LSU). Mature mammalian 55S mitochondrial ribosomes consist of
CC       a small (28S) and a large (39S) subunit. The 28S small subunit
CC       contains a 12S ribosomal RNA (12S mt-rRNA) and 30 different
CC       proteins. The 39S large subunit contains a 16S rRNA (16S mt-rRNA),
CC       a copy of mitochondrial valine transfer RNA (mt-tRNA(Val)), which
CC       plays an integral structural role, and 52 different proteins.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20186120, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23908630,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25278503, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25838379}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20186120,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23908630, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25278503,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25838379}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Down-regulated during the in vitro
CC       differentiation of HT29-D4 colon carcinoma cells.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8575443}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the prokaryotic/mitochondrial release
CC       factor family. Mitochondrion-specific ribosomal protein mL62
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: In contrast to other members of the family, lacks the
CC       regions that come into close contact with the mRNA in the
CC       ribosomal A-site and determine the STOP codon specificity,
CC       explaining the loss of codon specificity for translation release
CC       factor activity. {ECO:0000305}.
