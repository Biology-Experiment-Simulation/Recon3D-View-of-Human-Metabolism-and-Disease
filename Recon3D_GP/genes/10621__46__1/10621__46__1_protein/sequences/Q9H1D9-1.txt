CC   -!- FUNCTION: DNA-dependent RNA polymerase catalyzes the transcription
CC       of DNA into RNA using the four ribonucleoside triphosphates as
CC       substrates. Specific peripheric component of RNA polymerase III
CC       which synthesizes small RNAs, such as 5S rRNA and tRNAs. May
CC       direct RNA Pol III binding to the TFIIIB-DNA complex. Plays a key
CC       role in sensing and limiting infection by intracellular bacteria
CC       and DNA viruses. Acts as nuclear and cytosolic DNA sensor involved
CC       in innate immune response. Can sense non-self dsDNA that serves as
CC       template for transcription into dsRNA. The non-self RNA polymerase
CC       III transcripts, such as Epstein-Barr virus-encoded RNAs (EBERs)
CC       induce type I interferon and NF- Kappa-B through the RIG-I
CC       pathway. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19609254,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19631370}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the RNA polymerase III (Pol III) complex
CC       consisting of 17 subunits. RPC3/POLR3C, RPC6/POLR3F and
CC       RPC7/POLR3G form a Pol III subcomplex (By similarity). Interacts
CC       with TBP and TFIIIB90 and GTF3C4. {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10523658, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12391170}.
