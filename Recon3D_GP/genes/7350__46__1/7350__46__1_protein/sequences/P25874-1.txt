CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mitochondrial protein responsible for thermogenic
CC       respiration, a specialized capacity of brown adipose tissue and
CC       beige fat that participates to non-shivering adaptive
CC       thermogenesis to temperature and diet variations and more
CC       generally to the regulation of energy balance (By similarity).
CC       Functions as a long-chain fatty acid/LCFA and proton symporter,
CC       simultaneously transporting one LCFA and one proton through the
CC       inner mitochondrial membrane (PubMed:24196960). However, LCFAs
CC       remaining associated with the transporter via their hydrophobic
CC       tails, it results in an apparent transport of protons activated by
CC       LCFAs. Thereby, dissipates the mitochondrial proton gradient and
CC       converts the energy of substrate oxydation into heat instead of
CC       ATP. Regulates the production of reactive oxygen species/ROS by
CC       mitochondria (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P12242,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24196960}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Has no constitutive proton transporter activity
CC       and has to be activated by long-chain fatty acids/LCFAs. Inhibited
CC       by purine nucleotides. Both purine nucleotides and LCFAs bind the
CC       cytosolic side of the transporter and directly compete to activate
CC       or inhibit it. Activated by noradrenaline and reactive oxygen
CC       species. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P12242}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Most probably functions as a monomer. Binds one purine
CC       nucleotide per monomer (By similarity). However, has also been
CC       suggested to function as a homodimer or a homotetramer
CC       (PubMed:24196960). Tightly associates with cardiolipin in the
CC       mitochondrion inner membrane; may stabilize and regulate its
CC       activity (PubMed:24196960). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:W5PSH7,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24196960}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P12242}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P04633}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Brown adipose tissue.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:3165741}.
CC   -!- PTM: May undergo sulfenylation upon cold exposure. May increase
CC       the sensitivity of UCP1 thermogenic function to the activation by
CC       noradrenaline probably through structural effects.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P12242}.
CC   -!- PTM: May undergo ubiquitin-mediated proteasomal degradation.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P04633}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the mitochondrial carrier (TC 2.A.29)
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
