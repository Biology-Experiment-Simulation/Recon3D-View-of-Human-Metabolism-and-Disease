CC   -!- FUNCTION: DNA polymerase with very low fidelity that catalyzes
CC       considerable misincorporation by inserting dTTP opposite a G
CC       template, and dGTP opposite a T template (PubMed:16787914,
CC       PubMed:17118716). Is the least accurate of the DNA polymerase A
CC       family (i.e. POLG, POLN and POLQ) (PubMed:17118716). Can perform
CC       accurate translesion DNA synthesis (TLS) past a 5S-thymine glycol.
CC       Can perform efficient strand displacement past a nick or a gap and
CC       gives rise to an amount of product similar to that on non-damaged
CC       template. Has no exonuclease activity (PubMed:16787914). Error-
CC       prone DNA polymerase that preferentially misincorporates dT
CC       regardless of template sequence (PubMed:25775266). May play a role
CC       in TLS during interstrand cross-link (ICL) repair
CC       (PubMed:19908865). May be involved in TLS when genomic replication
CC       is blocked by extremely large major groove DNA lesions. May
CC       function in the bypass of some DNA-protein and DNA-DNA cross-
CC       links. May have a role in cellular tolerance to DNA cross-linking
CC       agents (PubMed:20102227). Involved in the repair of DNA cross-
CC       links and double-strand break (DSB) resistance. Participates in
CC       FANCD2-mediated repair. Forms a complex with HELQ helicase that
CC       participates in homologous recombination (HR) repair and is
CC       essential for cellular protection against DNA cross-links
CC       (PubMed:19995904). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16787914,
