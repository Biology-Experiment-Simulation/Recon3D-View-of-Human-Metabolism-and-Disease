CC   -!- FUNCTION: Retina-specific kinase involved in the signal turnoff
CC       via phosphorylation of rhodopsin (RHO), the G protein- coupled
CC       receptor that initiates the phototransduction cascade. This rapid
CC       desensitization is essential for scotopic vision and permits rapid
CC       adaptation to changes in illumination.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946941}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + [rhodopsin] = ADP + [rhodopsin]
CC       phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946941}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by phosphorylation of Ser-21.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946941}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=3.5 uM for rhodopsin {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946941};
CC         KM=10.6 uM for ATP {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946941};
CC         Vmax=1132 nmol/min/mg enzyme {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946941};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (when prenylated) with PDE6D; this promotes
CC       release from membranes. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28327}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28327};
CC       Lipid-anchor {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28327}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Retinal-specific. Expressed in rods and cones
CC       cells. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11717351}.
CC   -!- PTM: Autophosphorylated, Ser-21 is a minor site of
CC       autophosphorylation compared to Ser-491 and Thr-492 (By
CC       similarity). Phosphorylation at Ser-21 is regulated by light and
CC       activated by cAMP. {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946941}.
CC   -!- PTM: Farnesylation is required for full activity.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P28327}.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Night blindness, congenital stationary, Oguchi type 2
CC       (CSNBO2) [MIM:613411]: A non-progressive retinal disorder
CC       characterized by impaired night vision, often associated with
CC       nystagmus and myopia. Congenital stationary night blindness Oguchi
CC       type is associated with fundus discoloration and abnormally slow
CC       dark adaptation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17070587,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9020843}. Note=The disease is caused by
CC       mutations affecting the gene represented in this entry.
