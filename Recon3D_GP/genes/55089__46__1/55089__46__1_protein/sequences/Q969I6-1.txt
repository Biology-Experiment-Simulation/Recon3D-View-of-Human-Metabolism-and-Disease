CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sodium-dependent amino acid transporter. Mediates
CC       electrogenic symport of neutral amino acids and sodium ions. Has a
CC       broad specificity, with a preference for Ala, followed by His,
CC       Cys, Asn, Ser, Gly, Val, Thr, Gln and Met. May mediate sodium-
CC       independent transport of cationic amino acids, such as Arg and
CC       Lys. Amino acid uptake is pH-dependent, with low transport
CC       activities at pH 6.5, intermediate at pH 7.0 and highest between
CC       pH 7.5 and 8.5. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11342143,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11414754}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 7.5-8.5. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11342143};
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell membrane {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11414754,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16148032}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11414754, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16148032}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Detected in embryonic and adult liver, and at
CC       lower levels in adult muscle, kidney and pancreas. Detected in
CC       placenta syncytiotrophoblasts throughout gestation. Detected in
CC       fetal blood vessels. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11342143,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11414754, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16148032}.
CC   -!- PTM: The disulfide bond plays an important role in substrate
CC       transport, but has no effect on trafficking to the cell surface.
CC       {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the amino acid/polyamine transporter 2
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
