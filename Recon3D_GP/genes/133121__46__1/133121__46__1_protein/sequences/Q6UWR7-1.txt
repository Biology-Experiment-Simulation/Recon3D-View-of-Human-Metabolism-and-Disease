CC   -!- FUNCTION: Choline-specific glycerophosphodiester
CC       phosphodiesterase. The preferred substrate may be
CC       lysosphingomyelin (By similarity). Hydrolyzes
CC       lysophosphatidylcholine (LPC) to form monoacylglycerol and
CC       phosphorylcholine but not lysophosphatidic acid, showing it has a
CC       lysophospholipase C activity. Has a preference for LPC with short
CC       (12:0 and 14:0) or polyunsaturated (18:2 and 20:4) fatty acids.
CC       Also hydrolyzes glycerophosphorylcholine and
CC       sphingosylphosphorylcholine efficiently. Hydrolyzes the classical
CC       substrate for phospholipase C, p-nitrophenyl phosphorylcholine in
CC       vitro, while it does not hydrolyze the classical nucleotide
CC       phosphodiesterase substrate, p-nitrophenyl thymidine 5'-
CC       monophosphate. Does not hydrolyze diacyl phospholipids such as
CC       phosphatidylethanolamine, phosphatidylinositol,
CC       phosphatidylserine, phosphatidylglycerol and phosphatidic acid.
CC       {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15788404}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: A lysophosphatidylcholine + H(2)O = a
