CC   -!- FUNCTION: E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase that catalyzes 'Lys-48'- and
CC       'Lys-63'-linked polyubiquitin chains formation. Functions as an
CC       inhibitor of cytokine gene transcription. Inhibits IL2 and IL4
CC       transcription, thereby playing an important role in the induction
CC       of the anergic phenotype, a long-term stable state of T-lymphocyte
CC       unresponsiveness to antigenic stimulation associated with the
CC       blockade of interleukin production. Ubiquitinates ARPC5 with 'Lys-
CC       48' linkages and COR1A with 'Lys-63' linkages leading to their
CC       degradation, down-regulation of these cytosleletal components
CC       results in impaired lamellipodium formation and reduced
CC       accumulation of F-actin at the immunological synapse. Functions in
CC       the patterning of the dorsal ectoderm; sensitizes ectoderm to
CC       respond to neural-inducing signals. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12705856,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22016387}.
