CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in citrate-H(+)/malate exchange. Important for
CC       the bioenergetics of hepatic cells as it provides a carbon source
CC       for fatty acid and sterol biosyntheses, and NAD(+) for the
CC       glycolytic pathway.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion inner membrane; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Combined D-2- and L-2-hydroxyglutaric aciduria (D2L2AD)
CC       [MIM:615182]: An autosomal recessive neurometabolic disorder
CC       characterized by neonatal-onset encephalopathy with severe
CC       muscular weakness, intractable seizures, respiratory distress, and
CC       lack of psychomotor development resulting in early death. Brain
CC       imaging shows abnormalities including enlarged ventricles, delayed
CC       myelination, and germinal layer cysts.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23561848, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27306203}.
CC       Note=The disease is caused by mutations affecting the gene
CC       represented in this entry.
