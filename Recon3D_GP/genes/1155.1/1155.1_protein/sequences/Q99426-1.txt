CC   -!- FUNCTION: Binds to alpha-tubulin folding intermediates after their
CC       interaction with cytosolic chaperonin in the pathway leading from
CC       newly synthesized tubulin to properly folded heterodimer
CC       (PubMed:9265649). Involved in regulation of tubulin heterodimer
CC       dissociation. May function as a negative regulator of axonal
CC       growth (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D1E6,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9265649}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Supercomplex made of cofactors A to E. Cofactors A and D
CC       function by capturing and stabilizing tubulin in a quasi-native
CC       conformation. Cofactor E binds to the cofactor D-tubulin complex;
CC       interaction with cofactor C then causes the release of tubulin
CC       polypeptides that are committed to the native state
CC       (PubMed:9265649). Cofactors B and E can form a heterodimer which
CC       binds to alpha-tubulin and enhances their ability to dissociate
CC       tubulin heterodimers (By similarity). Interacts with GAN
CC       (PubMed:16303566). Interacts with DCTN1 (PubMed:22777741).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9D1E6, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16303566,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22777741, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9265649}.
