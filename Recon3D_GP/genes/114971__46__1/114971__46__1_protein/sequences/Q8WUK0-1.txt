CC   -!- FUNCTION: Lipid phosphatase which dephosphorylates
CC       phosphatidylglycerophosphate (PGP) to phosphatidylglycerol (PG).
CC       PGP is an essential intermediate in the biosynthetic pathway of
CC       cardiolipin, a mitochondrial-specific phospholipid regulating the
CC       membrane integrity and activities of the organelle. Has also been
CC       shown to display phosphatase activity toward phosphoprotein
CC       substrates, specifically mediates dephosphorylation of
CC       mitochondrial proteins, thereby playing an essential role in ATP
CC       production. Has probably a preference for proteins phosphorylated
CC       on Ser and/or Thr residues compared to proteins phosphorylated on
CC       Tyr residues. Probably involved in regulation of insulin secretion
CC       in pancreatic beta cells (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
