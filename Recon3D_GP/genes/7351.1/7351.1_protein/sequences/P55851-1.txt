CC   -!- FUNCTION: UCP are mitochondrial transporter proteins that create
CC       proton leaks across the inner mitochondrial membrane, thus
CC       uncoupling oxidative phosphorylation from ATP synthesis. As a
CC       result, energy is dissipated in the form of heat.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Acts as a dimer forming a proton channel. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P60409:KRTAP10-7; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-2842077, EBI-10172290;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion inner membrane; Multi-pass
CC       membrane protein.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Widely expressed in adult human tissues,
CC       including tissues rich in macrophages. Most expressed in white
CC       adipose tissue and skeletal muscle.
CC   -!- POLYMORPHISM: Genetic variations in UCP2 define the body mass
CC       index quantitative trait locus 4 (BMIQ4) [MIM:607447]. A common
CC       polymorphism in the promoter of UCP2 has been shown to be
CC       associated with a decreased risk of obesity in middle-aged
CC       individuals.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the mitochondrial carrier (TC 2.A.29)
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
