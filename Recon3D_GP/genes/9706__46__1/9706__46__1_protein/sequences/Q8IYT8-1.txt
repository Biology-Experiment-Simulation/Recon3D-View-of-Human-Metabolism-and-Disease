CC   -!- FUNCTION: Serine/threonine-protein kinase involved in autophagy in
CC       response to starvation. Acts upstream of phosphatidylinositol 3-
CC       kinase PIK3C3 to regulate the formation of autophagophores, the
CC       precursors of autophagosomes. Part of regulatory feedback loops in
CC       autophagy: acts both as a downstream effector and a negative
CC       regulator of mammalian target of rapamycin complex 1 (mTORC1) via
CC       interaction with RPTOR. Activated via phosphorylation by AMPK,
CC       also acts as a negative regulator of AMPK through phosphorylation
CC       of the AMPK subunits PRKAA1, PRKAB2 and PRKAG1. May phosphorylate
CC       ATG13/KIAA0652, FRS2, FRS3 and RPTOR; however such data need
CC       additional evidences. Not involved in ammonia-induced autophagy or
CC       in autophagic response of cerebellar granule neurons (CGN) to low
CC       potassium concentration. Plays a role early in neuronal
CC       differentiation and is required for granule cell axon formation:
CC       may govern axon formation via Ras-like GTPase signaling and
CC       through regulation of the Rab5-mediated endocytic pathways within
CC       developing axons. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18936157,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21460634, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21460635,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21690395, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21795849}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a protein = ADP + a phosphoprotein.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with SYNGAP1 (By similarity). Component of a
CC       complex consisting of ATG13/KIAA0652, ULK1 and RB1CC1/FIP200.
CC       Interacts (via C-terminus) with ATG13/KIAA0652. Associates with
CC       the mammalian target of rapamycin complex 1 (mTORC1) through an
CC       interaction with RPTOR. {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18936157,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21795849}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasmic vesicle membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18936157}; Peripheral membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18936157}. Note=Localizes to pre-
CC       autophagosomal membrane.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The CTD-like region mediates membrane-binding and
CC       incorporation into large protein complexes.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18936157}.
CC   -!- PTM: Autophosphorylated. In response to nutrient limitation,
CC       probably phosphorylated and activated by AMPK, leading to activate
CC       autophagy. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21205641}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the protein kinase superfamily. Ser/Thr
CC       protein kinase family. APG1/unc-51/ULK1 subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00159}.
