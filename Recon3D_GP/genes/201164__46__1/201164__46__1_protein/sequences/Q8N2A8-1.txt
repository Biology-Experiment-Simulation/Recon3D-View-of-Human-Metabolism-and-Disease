CC   -!- FUNCTION: Endonuclease that plays a critical role in PIWI-
CC       interacting RNA (piRNA) biogenesis during spermatogenesis. piRNAs
CC       provide essential protection against the activity of mobile
CC       genetic elements (By similarity). piRNA-mediated transposon
CC       silencing is thus critical for maintaining genome stability, in
CC       particular in germline cells when transposons are mobilized as a
CC       consequence of wide-spread genomic demethylation (By similarity).
CC       Has been proposed to act as a cardiolipin hydrolase to generate
CC       phosphatidic acid at mitochondrial surface (By similarity).
CC       Although it cannot be excluded that it can act as a phospholipase
CC       in some circumstances, it should be noted that cardiolipin
CC       hydrolase activity is either undetectable in vitro, or very low
CC       (PubMed:21397848). In addition, cardiolipin is almost exclusively
CC       found on the inner mitochondrial membrane, while PLD6 localizes to
CC       the outer mitochondrial membrane, facing the cytosol
CC       (PubMed:21397848). Has been shown to be a backbone-non-specific,
CC       single strand-specific nuclease, cleaving either RNA or DNA
CC       substrates with similar affinity. Produces 5' phosphate and 3'
CC       hydroxyl termini, suggesting it could directly participate in the
CC       processing of primary piRNA transcripts (By similarity). Also acts
CC       as a regulator of mitochondrial shape through facilitating
CC       mitochondrial fusion (PubMed:17028579, PubMed:26711011).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5SWZ9, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17028579,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21397848, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26711011}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: SsDNA hydrolase activity does not depend upon,
CC       but it stimulated by, the presence of Ca(2+) and Mn(2+).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5SWZ9}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:17028579). Interacts with MOV10L1 (By
CC       similarity). Interacts with MIGA1 and MIGA2; possibly facilitating
CC       homodimer formation (PubMed:26711011).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q5SWZ9, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17028579,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26711011}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Mitochondrion outer membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17028579}; Single-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17028579}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Widely expressed. Expression is not limited to
CC       testis and ovary. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17028579,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21397847}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: In contrast to other members of the phospholipase D
CC       family, contains only one PLD phosphodiesterase domain, suggesting
CC       that it has a single half-catalytic and requires homodimerization
CC       to form a complete active site. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17028579}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the phospholipase D family.
CC       MitoPLD/Zucchini subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
