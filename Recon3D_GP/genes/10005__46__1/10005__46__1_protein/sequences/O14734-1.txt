CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acyl-coenzyme A (acyl-CoA) thioesterases are a group of
CC       enzymes that catalyze the hydrolysis of acyl-CoAs to the free
CC       fatty acid and coenzyme A (CoASH), providing the potential to
CC       regulate intracellular levels of acyl-CoAs, free fatty acids and
CC       CoASH (PubMed:9299485, PubMed:9153233, PubMed:15194431). Competes
CC       with bile acid CoA:amino acid N-acyltransferase (BAAT) for bile
CC       acid-CoA substrate (such as chenodeoxycholoyl-CoA). Shows a
CC       preference for medium-length fatty acyl-CoAs (C2 to C20)
CC       (PubMed:9299485, PubMed:9153233). Inactive towards substrates with
CC       more than C20 aliphatic chains (PubMed:9153233). Involved in the
CC       metabolic regulation of peroxisome proliferation
CC       (PubMed:15194431). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15194431,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9153233, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9299485}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: (Microbial infection) May mediate Nef-induced down-
CC       regulation of CD4 cell-surface expression (PubMed:9153233).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9153233}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Choloyl-CoA + H(2)O = cholate + CoA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15194431, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9299485}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by CoASH (IC(50)=10-15 uM). Also
CC       inhibited by cysteine-reactive agents.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P58137}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=10.1 uM for decanoyl-CoA {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9153233};
CC         Vmax=7.1 umol/min/mg enzyme {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9153233};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P58137}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: (Microbial infection) Interacts with human
CC       immunodeficiency virus (HIV-1) Nef (via middle region); this
CC       interaction enhances ACOT8 Acyl-CoA thioesterase activity and
CC       occurs in a Nef myristoylation-independent manner
CC       (PubMed:9299485). According to a second report, the interaction
CC       with HIV-1 Nef occurs in a Nef myristoylation-independent manner
CC       but does not enhance ACOT8 Acyl-CoA thioesterase activity
CC       (PubMed:9153233). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9153233,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9299485}.
