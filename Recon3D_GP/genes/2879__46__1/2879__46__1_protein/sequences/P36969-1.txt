CC   -!- FUNCTION: Protects cells against membrane lipid peroxidation and
CC       cell death. Required for normal sperm development and male
CC       fertility. Could play a major role in protecting mammals from the
CC       toxicity of ingested lipid hydroperoxides. Essential for embryonic
CC       development. Protects from radiation and oxidative damage.
CC       Essential for maturation and survival of photoreceptor cells.
CC       Plays a role in a primary T cell response to viral and parasitic
CC       infection by protecting T cells from ferroptosis, a cell death
CC       resulting from an iron-dependent accumulation of lipid reactive
CC       oxygen species, and by supporting T cell expansion.
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:O70325}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 2 glutathione + a hydroperoxy-fatty-acyl-
