CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the last of the four reactions of the long-
CC       chain fatty acids elongation cycle. This endoplasmic reticulum-
CC       bound enzymatic process, allows the addition of 2 carbons to the
CC       chain of long- and very long-chain fatty acids/VLCFAs per cycle.
CC       This enzyme reduces the trans-2,3-enoyl-CoA fatty acid
CC       intermediate to an acyl-CoA that can be further elongated by
CC       entering a new cycle of elongation. Thereby, it participates in
CC       the production of VLCFAs of different chain lengths that are
CC       involved in multiple biological processes as precursors of
CC       membrane lipids and lipid mediators.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12482854}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: A very-long-chain acyl-CoA + NADP(+) = a very-
CC       long-chain trans-2,3-dehydroacyl-CoA + NADPH.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12482854}.
