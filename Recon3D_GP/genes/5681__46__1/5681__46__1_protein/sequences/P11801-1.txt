CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Golgi apparatus. Cytoplasm, cytoskeleton,
CC       microtubule organizing center, centrosome. Nucleus speckle.
CC       Endoplasmic reticulum membrane; Lipid-anchor. Cell membrane;
CC       Lipid-anchor. Cytoplasm. Note=Localized in the brefeldin A-
CC       sensitive Golgi compartment, at centrosomes, in the nucleus with a
CC       somewhat speckle-like presence, membrane-associated to the
CC       endoplasmic reticulum (ER) and the plasma membrane (PM), and more
CC       diffusely in the cytoplasm. Found to concentrate in splicing
CC       factor compartments (SFCs) within the nucleus of interphase cells.
CC       The acylation-negative form may be only cytoplasmic and nuclear.
CC       Acylation seems to allow the sequestering to the intracellular
CC       membranes. Myristoylation may mediate targeting to the
CC       intracellular non-Golgi membranes and palmitoylation may mediate
CC       the targeting to the Golgi membranes. Dual acylation is required
CC       to stabilize the interaction with Golgi membranes.

CC       with the highest level of abundance in testis.
