CC   -!- FUNCTION: Sulfotransferase that utilizes 3'-phospho-5'-adenylyl
CC       sulfate (PAPS) as sulfonate donor to catalyze the transfer of
CC       sulfate to position 6 of non-reducing N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc)
CC       residues within mucin-associated glycans that ultimately serve as
CC       SELL ligands. SELL ligands are present in high endothelial cells
CC       (HEVs) and play a central role in lymphocyte homing at sites of
CC       inflammation. Participates in biosynthesis of the SELL ligand
CC       sialyl 6-sulfo Lewis X on receptors SPN/CD43, GLYCAM1 and MADCAM1.
CC       Also involved in biosynthesis of SELL ligand recognized by MECA-79
CC       antibody. Plays a central role in lymphocyte trafficking during
CC       chronic inflammation. Has a catalytic preference for core 2-
CC       branched mucin-type O-glycans. Can use GlcNAcbeta1-6[Galbeta1-
CC       3]GalNAc-pNP (core 2), GlcNAcbeta1-6ManOMe and GlcNAcbeta1-2Man
CC       oligosaccharide structures as acceptors. Has also activity toward
CC       core 3 of GlcNAcbeta1-3GalNAc-pNP. Its substrate specificity may
CC       be influenced by its subcellular location.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10330415}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15220337}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Golgi apparatus membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12855678}; Single-pass type II membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12855678}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Specifically expressed in HEV. Weakly
CC       expressed in spleen. Not expressed in other tissues. Expressed in
CC       colonic mucinous adenocarcinoma. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10330415,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11310842, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12107080}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Upon cytokine activation, it is expressed at low level.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11310842}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: May serve as an antiinflammatory target.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the sulfotransferase 1 family.
CC       Gal/GlcNAc/GalNAc subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
