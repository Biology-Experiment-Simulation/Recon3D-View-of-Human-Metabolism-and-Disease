CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the first and rate-limiting reaction of the
CC       four that constitute the long-chain fatty acids elongation cycle.
CC       This endoplasmic reticulum-bound enzymatic process, allows the
CC       addition of 2 carbons to the chain of long- and very long-chain
CC       fatty acids/VLCFAs per cycle. Acts specifically toward
CC       polyunsaturated acyl-CoA with the higher activity toward C20:4(n-
CC       6) acyl-CoA. Condensing enzyme that catalyzes the synthesis of
CC       polyunsaturated very long chain fatty acid (C20- and C22-PUFA).
CC       May participate in the production of polyunsaturated VLCFAs of
CC       different chain lengths that are involved in multiple biological
CC       processes as precursors of membrane lipids and lipid mediators.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_03202, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12371743,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20937905}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: A very-long-chain acyl-CoA + malonyl-CoA = CoA
CC       + a very-long-chain 3-oxoacyl-CoA + CO(2). {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_03202, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12371743,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20937905}.
