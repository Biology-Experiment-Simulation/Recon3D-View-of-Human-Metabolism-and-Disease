CC   -!- FUNCTION: Uridylyltransferase that mediates the terminal
CC       uridylation of mRNAs with short (less than 25 nucleotides) poly(A)
CC       tails, hence facilitating global mRNA decay (PubMed:25480299).
CC       Involved in microRNA (miRNA)-induced gene silencing through
CC       uridylation of deadenylated miRNA targets. Also acts as a
CC       suppressor of miRNA biogenesis by mediating the terminal
CC       uridylation of some miRNA precursors, including that of let-7
CC       (pre-let-7), miR107, miR-143 and miR-200c. Uridylated miRNAs are
CC       not processed by Dicer and undergo degradation. Degradation of
CC       pre-let-7 contributes to the maintenance of embryonic stem (ES)
CC       cell pluripotency (By similarity). Does not bind RNA directly, but
CC       recruited to RNA targets by RNA-binding protein LIN28A. Also
CC       catalyzes the 3' uridylation of miR-26A, a miRNA that targets IL6
CC       transcript. This abrogates the silencing of IL6 transcript, hence
CC       promoting cytokine expression (By similarity). May also suppress
CC       Toll-like receptor-induced NF-kappa-B activation via binding to
CC       T2BP. Does not play a role in replication-dependent histone mRNA
CC       degradation. Due to functional redundancy between ZCCHC6 and
CC       ZCCHC11, the identification of the specific role of each of these
CC       proteins is difficult. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:B2RX14,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16643855, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19703396,
