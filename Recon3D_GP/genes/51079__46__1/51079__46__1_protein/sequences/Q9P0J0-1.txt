CC   -!- FUNCTION: Accessory subunit of the mitochondrial membrane
CC       respiratory chain NADH dehydrogenase (Complex I), that is believed
CC       not to be involved in catalysis. Complex I functions in the
CC       transfer of electrons from NADH to the respiratory chain. The
CC       immediate electron acceptor for the enzyme is believed to be
CC       ubiquinone. Involved in the interferon/all-trans-retinoic acid
CC       (IFN/RA) induced cell death. This apoptotic activity is inhibited
CC       by interaction with viral IRF1. Prevents the transactivation of
CC       STAT3 target genes. May play a role in CARD15-mediated innate
CC       mucosal responses and serve to regulate intestinal epithelial cell
CC       responses to microbes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12628925,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12867595, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15753091}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Complex I is composed of 45 different subunits. Interacts
CC       with CARD15, but not with CARD4. Interacts with STAT3, but not
CC       with STAT1, STAT2 and STAT5A. Interacts with HHV-8 IRF1, in the
CC       nucleus, with HPV-16 E6 and SV40 LT. Interacts with OLFM4.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12163600, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12611891,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12628925, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12867595,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15059901, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15753091}.
