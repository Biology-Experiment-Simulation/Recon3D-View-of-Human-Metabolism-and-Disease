CC   -!- FUNCTION: Plays a role in hippocampal-dependent synaptic
CC       plasticity, learning and memory. Involved in the formation of
CC       spines and functional synaptic contacts by modulating the
CC       translational activity of the cytoplasmic polyadenylation element-
CC       binding protein CPEB3. Promotes ubiquitination of CPEB3, and hence
CC       induces CPEB3-dependent mRNA translation activation of glutamate
CC       receptor GRIA1 and GRIA2. Can function as an E3 ubiquitin-protein
CC       ligase to activate monoubiquitination of JAG1 (in vitro), thereby
CC       regulating the Notch pathway. Acts as a tumor suppressor; inhibits
CC       malignant cell transformation of medulloblastoma (MB) cells by
CC       inhibiting the Notch signaling pathway.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20847082}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: S-ubiquitinyl-[E2 ubiquitin-conjugating
