CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acyltransferase that predominantly esterify long chain
CC       (wax) alcohols with acyl-CoA-derived fatty acids to produce wax
CC       esters. Wax esters are enriched in sebum, suggesting that it plays
CC       a central role in lipid metabolism in skin. Has no activity using
CC       decyl alcohol and significantly prefers the C16 and C18 alcohols.
CC       May also have 2-acylglycerol O-acyltransferase (MGAT) and acyl-
CC       CoA:retinol acyltransferase (ARAT) activities, to catalyze the
CC       synthesis of diacylglycerols and retinyl esters; however this
CC       activity is unclear in vivo. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15220349,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15671038, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16106050}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acyl-CoA + a long-chain alcohol = CoA + a
CC       long-chain ester. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16106050}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acyl-CoA + retinol = CoA + retinyl ester.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16106050}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         Vmax=1232 pmol/min/mg enzyme with [14C]cetyl-CoA as substrate
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15671038};
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15220349}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15220349}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Abundant in tissues rich in sebaceous glands
CC       such as the preputial gland and eyelid. Highly expressed in skin,
CC       where it is primarily restricted to undifferentiated peripheral
CC       sebocytes. Also expressed at lower level in other tissues except
CC       pancreas. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15220349,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15671038, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16106050}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the diacylglycerol acyltransferase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
