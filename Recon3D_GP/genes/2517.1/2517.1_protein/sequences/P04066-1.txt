CC   -!- FUNCTION: Alpha-L-fucosidase is responsible for hydrolyzing the
CC       alpha-1,6-linked fucose joined to the reducing-end N-
CC       acetylglucosamine of the carbohydrate moieties of glycoproteins.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: An alpha-L-fucoside + H(2)O = L-fucose + an
CC       alcohol. {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU10054}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Lysosome.
CC   -!- POLYMORPHISM: There are two common alleles of FUCA1; FUCA1*1; also
CC       known as Fu1; has Arg-281 and FUCA1*2; also known as Fu2; has Gln-
CC       281.
CC   -!- DISEASE: Fucosidosis (FUCA1D) [MIM:230000]: An autosomal recessive
CC       lysosomal storage disease characterized by accumulation of fucose-
CC       containing glycolipids and glycoproteins in various tissues.
CC       Clinical signs include facial dysmorphism, dysostosis multiplex,
CC       moderate hepatomegaly, severe intellectual deficit, deafness, and
CC       according to age, angiokeratomas. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7874128,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8504303, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9762612}. Note=The
CC       disease is caused by mutations affecting the gene represented in
CC       this entry.
