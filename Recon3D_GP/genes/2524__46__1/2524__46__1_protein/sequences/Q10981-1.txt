CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates the transfer of fucose to the terminal
CC       galactose on glycan chains of cell surface glycoproteins and
CC       glycolipids (PubMed:7876235). The resulting epitope plays a role
CC       in cell-cell interaction including host-microbe interaction
CC       (PubMed:12692541, PubMed:8018146). Mediates interaction with
CC       intestinal microbiota influencing its composition
CC       (PubMed:21625510, PubMed:24733310, PubMed:22068912). Creates a
CC       soluble precursor oligosaccharide FuC-alpha ((1,2)Galbeta-) called
CC       the H antigen which is an essential substrate for the final step
CC       in the soluble ABO blood group antigen synthesis pathway
CC       (PubMed:7876235). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21625510,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22068912, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24733310,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7876235, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8018146}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: GDP-beta-L-fucose + beta-D-galactosyl-(1->3)-

CC       beta-D-galactosyl-(1->3)-N-acetyl-beta-D-glucosaminyl-R.

CC   -!- POLYMORPHISM: Common polymorphisms in FUT2 define the vitamin B12
CC       plasma level quantitative trait locus 1 (B12QTL1) [MIM:612542].
CC       Vitamin B12 found in meat and milk products is necessary for the
CC       formation of red blood cells, DNA synthesis during cell division,
CC       and maintenance of the myelin nerve sheath, among other functions.
CC       Deficiency in vitamin B12, clinically associated with pernicious
CC       anemia, cardiovascular disease, cancer, and neurodegenerative
CC       disorders, is often related to poor intestinal B12 absorption
CC       rather than direct dietary deficiency.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18776911}.
CC   -!- POLYMORPHISM: Genetic variation in FUT2 results in the non-
CC       secretor phenotype which gives rise to non-functional FUT2,
CC       resulting in a lack of the H type-1 oligosaccharide ligand in
CC       secretions, and this prevents Norwalk virus binding contributing
CC       to resistance to Norwalk virus infection.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12692541}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: There are two genes (FUT1 and FUT2) which encode
CC       galactoside 2-L-fucosyltransferase. They are expressed in a
CC       tissue-specific manner with expression restricted to cells of
CC       mesodermal or endodermal origin respectively.
