CC   -!- FUNCTION: Riboflavin transporter. Riboflavin transport is Na(+)-
CC       independent but moderately pH-sensitive. Activity is strongly
CC       inhibited by riboflavin analogs, such as lumiflavin. Weakly
CC       inhibited by flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD). In case of
CC       infection by retroviruses, acts as a cell receptor to retroviral
CC       envelopes similar to the porcine endogenous retrovirus (PERV-A).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12740431, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20463145}.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Riboflavin deficiency (RBFVD) [MIM:615026]: A disorder
CC       caused by a primary defect in riboflavin metabolism, or by dietary
CC       riboflavin deficiency. Riboflavin deficiency during pregnancy
CC       results in hypoglycemia, metabolic acidosis, dicarboxylic aciduria
CC       and elevated plasma acylcarnitine levels in the newborn. Treatment
CC       with oral riboflavin results in complete resolution of the
CC       clinical and biochemical findings. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21089064}.
