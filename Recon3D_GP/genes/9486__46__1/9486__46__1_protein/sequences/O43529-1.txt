CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the transfer of sulfate to position 3 of
CC       terminal glucuronic acid of both protein- and lipid-linked
CC       oligosaccharides. Participates in biosynthesis of HNK-1
CC       carbohydrate structure, a sulfated glucuronyl-lactosaminyl residue
CC       carried by many neural recognition molecules, which is involved in
CC       cell interactions during ontogenetic development and in synaptic
CC       plasticity in the adult. May be indirectly involved in synapse
CC       plasticity of the hippocampus, via its role in HNK-1 biosynthesis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9478973}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Golgi apparatus membrane {ECO:0000250};
CC       Single-pass type II membrane protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: In fetal tissues, it is predominantly
CC       expressed in brain, and weakly expressed in lung, kidney and
CC       liver. In adult, it is highly expressed in brain, testis, ovary,
CC       expressed at intermediate level in heart, pancreas, skeletal
CC       muscle, spleen and thymus, and weakly expressed in other tissues.
CC       In brain, it is expressed at higher level in the frontal lobe.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9478973}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the sulfotransferase 2 family.
