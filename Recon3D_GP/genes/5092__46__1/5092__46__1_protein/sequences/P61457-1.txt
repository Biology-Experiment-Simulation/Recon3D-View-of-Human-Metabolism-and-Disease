CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in tetrahydrobiopterin biosynthesis. Seems to
CC       both prevent the formation of 7-pterins and accelerate the
CC       formation of quinonoid-BH2. Coactivator for HNF1A-dependent
CC       transcription. Regulates the dimerization of homeodomain protein
CC       HNF1A and enhances its transcriptional activity.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: (6R)-6-(L-erythro-1,2-dihydroxypropyl)-

CC       dihydroxypropyl)-7,8-dihydro-6H-pterin + H(2)O.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Hyperphenylalaninemia, BH4-deficient, D (HPABH4D)
CC       [MIM:264070]: An autosomal recessive disease characterized by
CC       primapterinuria, a variant form of hyperphenylalaninemia defined
CC       by increased excretion of 7-substituted pterins in the urine.
CC       Patients with primapterinuria show an increased ratio of neopterin
CC       to biopterin in the urine, excretion of subnormal levels of
CC       biopterins, and normal levels of biogenic amines in cerebrospinal
CC       fluid. Neurologic signs are mild, present in the neonatal period
CC       only, and include hypotonia, delayed motor development and tremor.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8352282, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9760199}. Note=The
CC       disease is caused by mutations affecting the gene represented in
CC       this entry.
