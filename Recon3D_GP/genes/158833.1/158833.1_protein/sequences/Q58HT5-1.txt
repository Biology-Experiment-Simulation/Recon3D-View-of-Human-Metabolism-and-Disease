CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acyltransferase that predominantly esterify long chain
CC       (wax) alcohols with acyl-CoA-derived fatty acids to produce wax
CC       esters. Wax esters are enriched in sebum, suggesting that it plays
CC       a central role in lipid metabolism in skin. Has a preference for
CC       arachidyl alcohol as well as decyl alcohol, demonstrating its
CC       relatively poor activity using saturated long chain alcohols (C16,
CC       C18, and C20). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15671038}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acyl-CoA + a long-chain alcohol = CoA + a
CC       long-chain ester.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         Vmax=31.7 pmol/min/mg enzyme with [14C]oleoyl-CoA as substrate
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15671038};
CC         Vmax=113 pmol/min/mg enzyme with [14C]cetyl-CoA as substrate
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15671038};
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Endoplasmic reticulum membrane
CC       {ECO:0000250}; Multi-pass membrane protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Predominantly expressed in skin, where it is
CC       limited to the sebaceous gland. Expressed in more mature,
CC       centrally located cells just before their rupture and sebum
CC       release. Also expressed in all tissues except spleen. Expressed at
CC       higher level in thymus, prostate and testis.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15671038}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the diacylglycerol acyltransferase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
