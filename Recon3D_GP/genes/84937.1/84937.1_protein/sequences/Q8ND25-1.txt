CC   -!- FUNCTION: E3 ubiquitin-protein ligase that mediates the
CC       ubiquitination of AKT1 and GLUL, thereby playing a role in neuron
CC       cells differentiation. Plays a role in the establishment and
CC       maintenance of neuronal transmission and plasticity. Regulates
CC       Schwann cells differentiation by mediating ubiquitination of GLUL.
CC       Promotes neurodegeneration by mediating 'Lys-48'-linked
CC       polyubiquitination and subsequent degradation of AKT1 in axons:
CC       degradation of AKT1 prevents AKT1-mediated phosphorylation of
CC       GSK3B, leading to GSK3B activation and phosphorylation of
CC       DPYSL2/CRMP2 followed by destabilization of microtubule assembly
CC       in axons (Probable). {ECO:0000305|PubMed:14561866}.
