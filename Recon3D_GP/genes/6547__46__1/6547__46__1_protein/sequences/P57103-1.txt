CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mediates the electrogenic exchange of Ca(2+) against
CC       Na(+) ions across the cell membrane, and thereby contributes to
CC       the regulation of cytoplasmic Ca(2+) levels and Ca(2+)-dependent
CC       cellular processes. Contributes to cellular Ca(2+) homeostasis in
CC       excitable cells, both in muscle and in brain. In a first phase,
CC       voltage-gated channels mediate the rapid increase of cytoplasmic
CC       Ca(2+) levels due to release of Ca(2+) stores from the endoplasmic
CC       reticulum. SLC8A3 mediates the export of Ca(2+) from the cell
CC       during the next phase, so that cytoplasmic Ca(2+) levels rapidly
CC       return to baseline. Contributes to Ca(2+) transport during
CC       excitation-contraction coupling in muscle. In neurons, contributes
CC       to the rapid decrease of cytoplasmic Ca(2+) levels back to
CC       baseline after neuronal activation, and thereby contributes to
CC       modulate synaptic plasticity, learning and memory (By similarity).
CC       Required for normal oligodendrocyte differentiation and for normal
CC       myelination (PubMed:21959935). Mediates Ca(2+) efflux from
CC       mitochondria and contributes to mitochondrial Ca(2+) ion
CC       homeostasis (By similarity). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:S4R2P9,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21959935}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Calcium transport is down-regulated by Na(+)
CC       and stimulated by Ca(2+). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P70549}.
