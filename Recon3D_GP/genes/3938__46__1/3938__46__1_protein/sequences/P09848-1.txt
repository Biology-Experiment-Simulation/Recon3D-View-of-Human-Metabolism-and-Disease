CC   -!- DISEASE: Congenital lactase deficiency (COLACD) [MIM:223000]:
CC       Autosomal recessive, rare and severe gastrointestinal disorder. It
CC       is characterized by watery diarrhea in infants fed with breast
CC       milk or other lactose-containing formulas. An almost total lack of
CC       LCT activity is found in jejunal biopsy material of patients with
CC       congenital lactase deficiency. Opposite to congenital lactase
CC       deficiency, also known as lactose intolerance, is the most common
CC       enzyme deficiency worldwide. It is caused by developmental down-
CC       regulation of lactase activity during childhood or early
CC       adulthood. The decline of lactase activity is a normal
CC       physiological phenomenon; however, the majority of Northern
CC       Europeans have the ability to maintain lactase activity and digest
CC       lactose throughout life (lactase persistence). The down-regulation
CC       of lactase activity operates at the transcriptional level and it
CC       is associated with a noncoding variation in the MCM6 gene, located
CC       in the upstream vicinity of LCT. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16400612}.
CC       Note=The disease is caused by mutations affecting the gene
CC       represented in this entry.
