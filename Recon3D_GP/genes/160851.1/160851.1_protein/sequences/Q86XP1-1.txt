CC   -!- FUNCTION: Phosphorylates diacylglycerol (DAG) to generate
CC       phosphatidic acid (PA). Plays a key role in promoting cell growth.
CC       Activates the Ras/B-Raf/C-Raf/MEK/ERK signaling pathway induced by
CC       EGF. Regulates the recruitment of RAF1 and BRAF from cytoplasm to
CC       membranes and their heterodimerization.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12810723, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19710016}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + 1,2-diacyl-sn-glycerol = ADP + 1,2-
CC       diacyl-sn-glycerol 3-phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12810723}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Isoform 1 forms homooligomers through the SAM domain.
CC       Isoform 1 is also able to form heterooligomers with SAM domain-
CC       containing isoforms of DGKD. Oligomerization of isoform 1 inhibits
CC       its catalytic activity. Interacts with RAF1 and BRAF.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12810723, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19710016}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12810723,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19710016}. Cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12810723, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19710016}.
CC       Note=Translocated from the cytoplasm to endosomes in response to
CC       stress stimuli (PubMed:12810723). Isoform 2 is rapidly relocated
CC       back to the cytoplasm upon removal of stress stimuli, whereas
CC       isoform 1 exhibits sustained endosomal association
CC       (PubMed:12810723). Translocates from the cytoplasm to the cell
CC       membrane in the presence of active GTP-bound form of HRAS
CC       (PubMed:19710016).
