CC       Functions as a pH sensing receptor which is activated by increased
CC       extracellular pH. Activates an intracellular signaling pathway
CC       that involves IRS1 and AKT1/PKB. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21641549}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a [protein]-L-tyrosine = ADP + a
CC       [protein]-L-tyrosine phosphate. {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-
CC       ProRule:PRU10028}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Probable tetramer of 2 alpha and 2 beta chains linked by
CC       disulfide bonds. The alpha chains contribute to the formation of
CC       the ligand-binding domain, while the beta chains carry the kinase
CC       domain.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane; Single-pass type I membrane
CC       protein.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The extracellular domain is required for sensing
CC       alterations in external pH.

CC       10.5.
