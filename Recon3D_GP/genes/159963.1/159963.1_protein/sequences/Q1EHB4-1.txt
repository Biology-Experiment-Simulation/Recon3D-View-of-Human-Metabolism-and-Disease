CC   -!- FUNCTION: Acts as an electroneutral and low-affinity sodium
CC       (Na(+))-dependent sodium-coupled solute transporter. Catalyzes the
CC       transport across the plasma membrane of many monocarboxylates such
CC       as lactate, pyruvate, nicotinate, propionate, butyrate and beta-D-
CC       hydroxybutyrate. May be responsible for the first step of
CC       reabsorption of monocarboxylates from the lumen of the proximal
CC       tubule of the kidney and the small intestine. May play also a role
CC       in monocarboxylates transport in the retina (By similarity).
CC       Mediates electroneutral uptake of lactate, with a stoichiometry of
CC       2 Na(+) for each lactate (By similarity). {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17692818}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Cotransport of monocarboxylates and nicotinate
CC       strongly inhibited by ibuprofen, fenoprofen and ketoprofen.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Apical cell membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17692818}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17692818}. Note=Detected at the brush border
CC       membrane of the kidney. Colocalizes with viementin in Mueller
CC       cells (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
