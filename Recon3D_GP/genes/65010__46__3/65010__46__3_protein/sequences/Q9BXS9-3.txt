CC   -!- FUNCTION: Apical membrane anion-exchanger with wide epithelial
CC       distribution that plays a role as a component of the pH buffering
CC       system for maintaining acid-base homeostasis. Acts as a versatile
CC       DIDS-sensitive inorganic and organic anion transporter that
CC       mediates the uptake of monovalent anions like chloride,
CC       bicarbonate, formate and hydroxyl ion and divalent anions like
CC       sulfate and oxalate. Function in multiple exchange modes involving
CC       pairs of these anions, which include chloride-bicarbonate,
CC       chloride-oxalate, oxalate-formate, oxalate-sulfate and chloride-
CC       formate exchange. Apical membrane chloride-bicarbonate exchanger
CC       that mediates luminal chloride absorption and bicarbonate
CC       secretion by the small intestinal brush border membrane and
CC       contributes to intracellular pH regulation in the duodenal upper
CC       villous epithelium during proton-coupled peptide absorption,
CC       possibly by providing a bicarbonate import pathway. Mediates also
CC       intestinal chloride absorption and oxalate secretion, thereby
CC       preventing hyperoxaluria and calcium oxalate urolithiasis.
CC       Transepithelial oxalate secretion, chloride-formate, chloride-
CC       oxalate and chloride-bicarbonate transport activities in the
CC       duodenum are inhibited by PKC activation in a calcium-independent
CC       manner. The apical membrane chloride-bicarbonate exchanger
CC       provides also a major route for fluid and bicarbonate secretion
CC       into the proximal tubules of the kidney as well as into the
CC       proximal part of the interlobular pancreatic ductal tree, where it
CC       mediates electrogenic chloride-bicarbonate exchange with a
CC       chloride-bicarbonate stoichiometry of 1:2, and hence will dilute
CC       and alkalinize protein-rich acinar secretion. Mediates also the
CC       transcellular sulfate absorption and oxalate secretion across the
CC       apical membrane in the duodenum and the formate ion efflux at the
CC       apical brush border of cells in the proximal tubules of kidney.
CC       Plays a role in sperm capacitation by increasing intracellular pH.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Isoform 4: Apical membrane chloride-bicarbonate
CC       exchanger. Its association with carbonic anhydrase CA2 forms a
CC       bicarbonate transport metabolon; hence maximizes the local
CC       concentration of bicarbonate at the transporter site.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Apical membrane chloride-formate exchange
CC       activity in the proximal tubules of the kidney and oxalate
CC       secretion in the duodenum are inhibited by 4,4'-
CC       diisothiocyanatostilbene-2,2'-disulfonic acid (DIDS - an inhibitor
CC       of several anion channels and transporters) (By similarity).
CC       Apical membrane chloride-bicarbonate exchange activity of the
CC       pancreatic duct is inhibited by 4,4'-diisothiocyanatostilbene-
CC       2,2'-disulfonic acid (DIDS - an inhibitor of several anion
CC       channels and transporters). Isoform 4, isoform 5 and isoform 6
CC       chloride, bicarbonate and sulfate transport activities are
CC       inhibited by DIDS. {ECO:0000250, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12444019,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15990874, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20501439}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts (via C-terminal domain) with PDZK1 (via C-
CC       terminal PDZ domain); the interaction induces chloride and oxalate
CC       exchange transport. Interacts with CFTR, SLC26A3 and SLC9A3R1 (By
CC       similarity). Isoform 4 interacts (via C-terminal cytoplasmic
CC       domain) with CA2; the interaction stimulates chloride-bicarbonate
CC       exchange activity. Isoform 4 and isoform 5 interact with SLC9A3R1
CC       (via the PDZ domains). Isoform 4 and isoform 5 interact with
CC       SLC9A3R2 (via the PDZ domains). Interacts with AHCYL1; the
CC       interaction increases SLC26A6 activity (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q9BXS9, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12444019,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15990874}.
