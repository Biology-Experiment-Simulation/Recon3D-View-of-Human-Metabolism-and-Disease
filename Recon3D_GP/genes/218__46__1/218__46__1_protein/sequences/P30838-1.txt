CC   -!- FUNCTION: ALDHs play a major role in the detoxification of
CC       alcohol-derived acetaldehyde (Probable). They are involved in the
CC       metabolism of corticosteroids, biogenic amines, neurotransmitters,
CC       and lipid peroxidation (Probable). Oxidizes medium and long chain
CC       aldehydes into non-toxic fatty acids (PubMed:1737758).
CC       Preferentially oxidizes aromatic aldehyde substrates
CC       (PubMed:1737758). Comprises about 50 percent of corneal epithelial
CC       soluble proteins (By similarity). May play a role in preventing
CC       corneal damage caused by ultraviolet light (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P47739, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1737758,
CC       ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: An aldehyde + NAD(P)(+) + H(2)O = a
CC       carboxylate + NAD(P)H. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1737758,
