CC   -!- FUNCTION: Creates a soluble precursor oligosaccharide FuC-alpha
CC       ((1,2)Gal-beta-) called the H antigen which is an essential
CC       substrate for the final step in the soluble A and B antigen
CC       synthesis pathway. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2118655}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: GDP-beta-L-fucose + beta-D-galactosyl-(1->4)-
CC       N-acetyl-beta-D-glucosaminyl-R = GDP + alpha-L-fucosyl-(1->2)-
CC       beta-D-galactosyl-(1->4)-N-acetyl-beta-D-glucosaminyl-R.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2118655}.

CC   -!- PATHWAY: Protein modification; protein glycosylation.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Golgi apparatus, Golgi stack membrane;
CC       Single-pass type II membrane protein. Note=Membrane-bound form in
CC       trans cisternae of Golgi.
CC   -!- POLYMORPHISM: Genetic variations in FUT1 define the H blood group
CC       and are responsible for the Bombay and para-Bombay phenotypes
CC       [MIM:616754]. Erythrocytes from individuals with the Bombay and
CC       para-Bombay blood group phenotypes are deficient in H antigens.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7912436, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9122901,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9226185, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9299444}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: There are two genes (FUT1 and FUT2) which encode
CC       galactoside 2-L-fucosyltransferase. They are expressed in a
CC       tissue-specific manner with expression restricted to cells of
CC       mesodermal or endodermal origin respectively.
