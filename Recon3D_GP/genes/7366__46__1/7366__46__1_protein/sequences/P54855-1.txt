CC   -!- FUNCTION: UDPGTs are of major importance in the conjugation and
CC       subsequent elimination of potentially toxic xenobiotics and
CC       endogenous compounds. This isozyme displays activity toward
CC       several classes of xenobiotic substrates, including simple
CC       phenolic compounds, 7-hydroxylated coumarins, flavonoids,
CC       anthraquinones, and certain drugs and their hydroxylated
CC       metabolites. It also catalyzes the glucuronidation of endogenous
CC       estrogens and androgens.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: UDP-glucuronate + acceptor = UDP + acceptor
CC       beta-D-glucuronoside.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Microsome membrane {ECO:0000305}; Single-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000305}. Endoplasmic reticulum
CC       membrane {ECO:0000305}; Single-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in many tissues. Present in liver,
CC       prostate and testis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8399210}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the UDP-glycosyltransferase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
