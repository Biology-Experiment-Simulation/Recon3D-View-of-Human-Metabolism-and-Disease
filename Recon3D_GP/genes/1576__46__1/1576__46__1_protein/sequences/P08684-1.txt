CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cytochromes P450 are a group of heme-thiolate
CC       monooxygenases. In liver microsomes, this enzyme is involved in an
CC       NADPH-dependent electron transport pathway. It performs a variety
CC       of oxidation reactions (e.g. caffeine 8-oxidation, omeprazole
CC       sulphoxidation, midazolam 1'-hydroxylation and midazolam 4-
CC       hydroxylation) of structurally unrelated compounds, including
CC       steroids, fatty acids, and xenobiotics. Acts as a 1,8-cineole 2-
CC       exo-monooxygenase. The enzyme also hydroxylates etoposide
CC       (PubMed:11159812). Catalyzes 4-beta-hydroxylation of cholesterol.
CC       May catalyze 25-hydroxylation of cholesterol in vitro
CC       (PubMed:21576599). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11159812,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21576599}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Quinine + NADPH + O(2) = 3-hydroxyquinine +
CC       NADP(+) + H(2)O. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11159812}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Taurochenodeoxycholate + NADPH + O(2) =
CC       taurohyocholate + NADP(+) + H(2)O. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11159812}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Lithocholate + NADPH + O(2) = hyodeoxycholate
CC       + NADP(+) + H(2)O. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11159812}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Albendazole + NADPH + O(2) = albendazole S-
CC       oxide + NADP(+) + H(2)O. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11159812}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 1,8-cineole + NADPH + O(2) = 2-exo-hydroxy-
CC       1,8-cineole + NADP(+) + H(2)O. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11159812}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: RH + [reduced NADPH--hemoprotein reductase] +
CC       O(2) = ROH + [oxidized NADPH--hemoprotein reductase] + H(2)O.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21576599}.

CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Expressed in prostate and liver. According to
CC       some authors, it is not expressed in brain (PubMed:19094056).
CC       According to others, weak levels of expression are measured in
CC       some brain locations (PubMed:19359404 and PubMed:18545703). Also
CC       expressed in epithelium of the small intestine and large
CC       intestine, bile duct, nasal mucosa, kidney, adrenal cortex,
CC       epithelium of the gastric mucosa with intestinal metaplasia,
CC       gallbladder, intercalated ducts of the pancreas, chief cells of
CC       the parathyroid and the corpus luteum of the ovary (at protein
CC       level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18545703, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19094056,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19359404, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2732228,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7894497, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8035341}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By glucocorticoids. Also induced to high levels in
CC       liver and other tissues by various foreign compounds, including
CC       drugs, pesticides, and carcinogens.
CC   -!- PTM: Polyubiquitinated in the presence of AMFR and UBE2G1 and also
CC       STUB1/CHIP and UBE2D1 (in vitro). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19103148}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Chimeric transcripts, characterized by CYP3A43 exon
CC       1 joined at canonical splice sites to distinct sets of CYP3A4
CC       exons, have been detected. All are possibly produced by trans-
CC       splicing. The chimeric transcripts exist in 3 different
CC       combinations: CYP3A43 exon 1 joined in frame to CYP3A4 exons 2-13,
CC       CYP3A43 exon 1 joined in frame to CYP3A4 exons 4-13 and CYP3A43
CC       exon 1 joined in frame to CYP3A4 exon 7-13. The longest chimeric
CC       isoform (CYP3A43 exon 1 joined to CYP3A4 exons 2-13) exhibits 6-
CC       beta-hydroxylase activity, while a shorter isoform (CYP3A43 exon 1
CC       joined to CYP3A4 exons 4-13) does not. All chimeric transcripts
CC       are expressed at very low levels in the liver (PubMed:11726664).
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11726664}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the cytochrome P450 family. {ECO:0000305}.
