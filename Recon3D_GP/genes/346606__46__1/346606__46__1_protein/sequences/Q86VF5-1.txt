CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the formation of diacylglycerol from 2-
CC       monoacylglycerol and fatty acyl-CoA. Also able to catalyze the
CC       terminal step in triacylglycerol synthesis by using diacylglycerol
CC       and fatty acyl-CoA as substrates. Has a preference toward
CC       palmitoyl-CoA and oleoyl-CoA. May be involved in absorption of
CC       dietary fat in the small intestine by catalyzing the resynthesis
CC       of triacylglycerol in enterocytes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12618427}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acyl-CoA + 2-acylglycerol = CoA +
CC       diacylglycerol. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12618427}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acyl-CoA + 1,2-diacylglycerol = CoA +
CC       triacylglycerol. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12618427}.
